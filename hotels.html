Top Hotels in Scotland

The Balmoral, Edinburgh

History: Opened in 1902 as the North British Station Hotel, The Balmoral has become an icon of Edinburgh. Its clock tower is traditionally set three minutes fast to help travelers catch their trains.

Description: A 5-star luxury hotel located in the heart of the city, offering elegant rooms, a Michelin-starred restaurant, and stunning views of Edinburgh Castle.

Gleneagles Hotel, Auchterarder

History: Opened in 1924, Gleneagles quickly became one of the most prestigious resorts in the UK. It has hosted the G8 summit and golf tournaments, including the Ryder Cup.

Description: A renowned golf resort with three championship courses, offering a luxury spa, fine dining, and a wide range of activities from horseback riding to falconry.

Dalhousie Castle Hotel & Spa, Bonnyrigg

History: Dating back to the 13th century, the castle belonged to the Ramsay clan for over 700 years. Today, it is one of the oldest castles in Scotland operating as a hotel.

Description: Unique historically styled rooms, a dungeon restaurant, and a luxury spa. Guests can also enjoy falconry and archery on the grounds.

Inverlochy Castle, Fort William

History: Built in the 19th century, Inverlochy Castle hosted Queen Victoria, who admired its beauty. The hotel is located at the foot of Ben Nevis, the highest mountain in the UK.

Description: A boutique hotel offering exclusive rooms, exquisite cuisine by renowned chef Albert Roux, and unparalleled views of the Scottish countryside.

The Witchery by the Castle, Edinburgh

History: Located in a 16th-century building next to Edinburgh Castle, the hotel's name refers to the witch hunts that took place in the area.

Description: Incredibly atmospheric, luxurious suites decorated in a gothic style, with rich decorations, heavy curtains, and antique furniture. Perfect for couples seeking a romantic atmosphere.

Fonab Castle Hotel, Pitlochry

History: The castle, built in 1892 as a noble residence, was transformed into a luxury hotel in 2013.

Description: Located by a lake, it combines the traditional character of a castle with modern interior design. Offers a spa, fine dining, and picturesque views of the Scottish landscape.

Skibo Castle, Dornoch

History: Originally a medieval residence, it was restored by Andrew Carnegie in the early 20th century as his private estate.

Description: Now operating as an exclusive members' club, offering luxurious interiors, a golf course, and a wide range of outdoor activities.
